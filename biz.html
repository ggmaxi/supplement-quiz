HOW IT WORKS

Quiz → Protocol → Purchase

A clean three-step funnel. We sit at the top — the most defensible position — and let brand partners handle fulfilment.

01
User takes the 5-question quiz
Goals, lifestyle, diet, specific concerns, and budget. Takes under 2 minutes. No email required to get results (removes friction, builds trust).
02
AI generates a personalized protocol
5 supplements ranked by priority — each with a plain-English "why" and exact dosage. Evidence-based, Canada-aware (Vitamin D for our winters, etc.). No upsells, no filler.
03
User clicks through to buy
Each recommendation links to a vetted Canadian retailer (Popeye's, Natural Health Trends, iHerb.ca, Amazon.ca) via an affiliate link. We earn 8–15% on every order.
04
Content compounds the flywheel
SEO articles ("best magnesium supplement Canada") rank and refer traffic organically. Every article is another affiliate revenue stream that runs forever with zero marginal cost.

THE EDGE

The supplement market is saturated with products. It's empty on trusted, unbiased curation.

🍁
Canada-first positioning
US brands dominate online content. Canadian-specific advice (retailers, regulations, sun/vitamin D reality) is genuinely underserved. Own the niche before anyone notices.
🏛️
Zero conflict of interest
We don't make supplements. Our only incentive is accurate recommendations. This is the actual value prop — and it's rare. Care/of destroyed trust by going vertical.

Content compounds forever
A good SEO article from year one still drives revenue in year five. Affiliate links don't expire. Unlike ads, you're building an asset, not renting attention.
🤖

Generic "top 10 supplements" articles convert poorly. A quiz that generates *your* protocol — named, dosed, explained — converts at 2–4x the rate of static content.

No inventory, ever
Care/of raised $156M and burned it on manufacturing + subscription logistics. We avoid both failure modes. Gross margin is 90%+ until we choose otherwise.
🧱
Low cost to start, hard to kill
~$5K to launch properly. No suppliers, no warehouse, no expiry dates. Once the content machine runs, this business doesn't need much attention to keep earning.
GO-TO-MARKET

How we get distribution

This is a media + affiliate business, not a DTC brand. The GTM playbook is closer to Wirecutter than to a supplement company.

Phase 0 — Before You Drive Traffic Week 1

Get conversion infrastructure right before pushing traffic. A leaky funnel at 10 visitors is a leaky funnel at 10,000.

🔗
Wire affiliate programs
iHerb Canada (8%), Amazon.ca Associates (6–10%), Popeye's Supplements, Natural Health Trends. Free to join — do it this week.
📧
Add email capture to the results page
"Email me my protocol." Single field. The email list is the one asset you fully own — affiliate links change, social accounts get banned, email doesn't.
📊
Set up analytics
Plausible or GA4. Track: traffic source → quiz completion → affiliate click. You need to know what converts before scaling anything.
🌐
Get a real domain
stacksmart.ca or similar. GitHub Pages is a prototype URL. A .ca domain also signals Canadian relevance to Google — matters for SEO.

Phase 3 — Paid Distribution Month 4+, only after organic works

Don't touch paid until you have 100+ organic quiz completions and a known affiliate conversion rate.

🎯

Google Search — bottom-of-funnel only
Bid on keywords like "buy magnesium glycinate Canada" — someone already decided, just needs pointing. Converts 5–10x vs. awareness keywords. Avoid broad match.

⚠️ What NOT To Do

Don't spread across every platform
Pick one short-form (TikTok or Reels) and one long-form (X or YouTube). Go deep before expanding. Spreading thin = mediocre everywhere.
Don't publish AI slop for SEO
Google's helpful content update nuked thin affiliate sites. The edge is being genuinely better — real experience, Canadian specifics, honest opinions.
Don't chase virality
A viral post sends 50K people who bounce. Ten good SEO articles send 500 people/month who buy. The model rewards compounding, not spikes.
Don't pitch sponsorships too early
Reaching out at <2K visitors trains brands to think your audience is small. Wait until the number is real. One good deal at 5K > five rejections at 500.
